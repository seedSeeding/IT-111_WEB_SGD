Glaciers

Glaciers are large, slow-moving masses of ice formed from accumulated snow, which gradually compresses over time and shapes the landscape as they flow.

Heatwaves Glaciers are critical reserves of fresh water, especially for seasonal meltwater users. Their disappearance imperils water supplies, agriculture, and hydroelectric power, mainly during dry seasons. Sea level rise, caused by melting ice caps and thermal expansion of seawater, threatens the coastal and island communities. Rising waters flood low-lying areas, displacing populations, damaging infrastructure, and contaminating freshwater supplies with saltwater. This phenomenon increases vulnerability for many regions globally.

What to do

Use skis or snowshoes

These distribute your weight across a larger surface area, which can help you cross crevasses without sinking.

Use ski poles

You can use the basket as a probe to test if you're over a crevasse. If the pole only compacts a few centimeters, it's likely safe to stand there.

Be aware of crevasses

The upper layers of a glacier are more brittle and can fracture, forming huge crevasses that are sometimes covered by snow.
