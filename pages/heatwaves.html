Heawaves

Heatwaves are prolonged periods of abnormally high temperatures

Heatwaves are extended periods of hot weather that are usually associated with humidity. They have a major impact on human health, causing heat exhaustion, heatstroke, and cardiac strain, particularly among vulnerable groups such as the elderly and children. Economically, they increase energy demand because people rely on air conditioning, straining power grids and increasing costs. They also affect agriculture, causing crop failures and food price inflation.

What to do

Stay hydrated

drink water regularly, at least 1 cup of water every hour, and avoid some alcohol drink.

Stay cool

wear light, loose-fitting clothing and wide-brimmed hat. Take cool showers or baths, or wet some of your skin with a damp cloth.

Avoid the heat

Stay indoors during the hottest part of the day, which is ussually between 10 AM until 2 Pm. If you want to go outside, you must be wear shade or sunscreen.
