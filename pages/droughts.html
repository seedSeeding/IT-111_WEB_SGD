Droughts

Droughts are prolonged periods of abnormally low rainfall, leading to water shortages and adverse impacts on agriculture, ecosystems, and livelihoods.

Droughts is a prolonged dry period in the natural climate cycle that can occur anywhere in the world. It is a slow-onset disaster characterized by the lack of precipitation, resulting in a water shortage. Drought can have a serious impact on health, agriculture, economies, energy and the environment. An estimated 55 million people globally are affected by droughts every year, and they are the most serious hazard to livestock and crops in nearly every part of the world. Drought threatens people’s livelihoods, increases the risk of disease and death, and fuels mass migration. Water scarcity impacts 40% of the world’s population, and as many as 700 million people are at-risk of being displaced as a result of drought by 2030. Rising temperatures caused by climate change are making already dry regions drier and wet regions wetter. In dry regions, this means that when temperatures rise, water evaporates more quickly, and thus increases the risk of drought or prolongs periods of drought. Between 80-90% of all documented disasters from natural hazards during the past 10 years have resulted from floods, droughts, tropical cyclones, heat waves and severe storms.

What to do

Conserve water

Limit how much water each person uses, and don't flush the toilet unless necessary. Take short showers instead of baths, and use bottled water when possible.

Fix leaks

Check your plumbing regularly and fix any leaks as soon as you find them.

Install water-saving fixtures

Install aerators with flow restrictors on faucets, and consider a low-volume toilet.

Use efficient appliances

Choose appliances that are more energy and water efficient.

Harvest rainwater

Rainwater harvesting can help combat drought. Use drip irrigation: Drip irrigation systems deliver water directly to plant roots, preventing waste.

Use sustainable agriculture

Plant drought-tolerant crops, and use mulch and crop covers to retain water. You can also use animal compost and plant manures to increase soil fertility.

Use permeable materials

Lay green driveways and water-permeable asphalt instead of concrete.
